hw0

I am a first year PhD student at NYU Computer Science Department. My research interests lie in graphics, animation, augmented reality and interfaces.

Prior to NYU, I completed an M.S. in Computer Science at Pontificia Universidad Catolica of Chile (PUC), advised by Prof. Miguel Nussbuam. I did my thesis on “Augmented Reality in collaborative games for physics education”.

why computer graphics?

During my years at PUC I explored the different areas of computer science searching for a field where I could work with structure and logic, and also with movement, colors and other visual elements. I took courses on image manipulation, computer vision, computer graphics, interfaces and games. Today I believe that somewhere between these areas lies my ideal research area.

As you see, I’m not sure what is going to be my specific area of research. Now days I’m trying to think in new ways in which CS can support creative and artistic processes.

On fall-2013 I’m taking the “Computer Graphics” course dictated by Prof. Perlin. During the course of the semester I hope to get more familiar with computer graphics fundamentals: ray tracing, surfaces, splines, texture mapping, etc.

I believe the course will give me tools to work on CG, and will help me understand the multiple applications it can have. I took a similar class in PUC, and I believe going over some old and new topics will be a great start for my PhD.
